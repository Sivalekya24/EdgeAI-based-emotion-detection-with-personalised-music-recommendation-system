Under the Hood

How the System Works

MoodWave uses a deep convolutional neural network to analyse your facial expression and detect the dominant emotion from 7 classes.

The model evaluates image patterns, textures, edge behaviour, and lighting to extract precise emotion features from your face.

Validation layers check brightness, alignment, and head stability before inference — ensuring only high-quality frames are analysed.

Guidelines

Tips for Best Results

Follow these guidelines for the most accurate emotion detection from MoodWave.

😊
1
Single Face Only

Position one face clearly in the centre of the frame. Ensure no other faces appear in the background for clean detection.

💡
2
Good Lighting

Ensure your face is well-lit from the front. Avoid backlighting or dim rooms — brightness is validated before detection.

😠
3
Show Clear Emotion

Exaggerate your expression slightly — especially for subtle emotions like sadness or disgust. The CNN needs clear visual cues.

📐
4
Correct Distance

Sit 40–70 cm from the screen. Your face should fill roughly one-third of the camera view for optimal detection accuracy.

🧘
5
Stay Still & Straight

Head tilt and movement are checked for stability. Keep your head level and avoid tilting more than a few degrees.

🔒
6
Privacy First

Camera stops the instant you capture. One frozen frame is sent to the local server only — nothing is stored or uploaded.

Technology

Powered by Edge AI

A complete on-device pipeline from face capture to personalised playlist.

🧠

CNN Emotion Model

A deep convolutional neural network classifies 7 emotions from facial expressions with high accuracy and speed.

⚡

Edge Inference

All inference runs on your local Flask server — no cloud calls, no third-party AI APIs. Your face never leaves your machine.

✅

Smart Validation

Brightness, face-area, alignment and stability checks ensure only clean frames reach the model before inference.

Confidence Scoring

All 7 emotion probabilities computed via softmax. The highest confidence class becomes the final emotion output.
